About Our Major Project

📱 Smartphone-Integrated Micro-Volume Nephelometer

Core Concept: Developing a portable nephelometer system that uses a smartphone camera to measure turbidity or suspended particle concentration in liquids through light scattering analysis. It uses a low-cost optical setup, a sealed disposable micro-volume cartridge, and smartphone-based image processing to create an accessible alternative to traditional laboratory nephelometers.

⚙️ System Architecture

  • Optical Illumination: Monochromatic LED light source for stable, fixed-geometry illumination.
  • Micro-Volume Cartridge: Custom disposable sealed cartridge to prevent contamination and ensure consistent optical conditions.
  • Opto-Mechanical Housing: 3D-printed enclosure to align the LED, sample, and smartphone camera (at a 90° scattering angle) while blocking ambient light.
  • Image Processing Software: A custom pipeline to capture images, extract pixel intensity, apply background correction, and quantify scattering intensity into turbidity metrics.

🎯 Key Design Objectives

  1. Ensure optical illumination stability for repeatable measurements.
  2. Maintain controlled scattering geometry (90° fixed angle).
  3. Design a sealed micro-volume cartridge for uniform conditions.
  4. Precision opto-mechanical alignment via 3D printing.
  5. Total ambient light isolation.
  6. Develop robust image processing algorithms to quantify scattered light.
  7. Establish a calibration curve linking intensity to actual particle concentration.
