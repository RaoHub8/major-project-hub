About Our Major Project

📱 Smartphone-Integrated Micro-Volume Nephelometer

Project Type: Final Year Major Engineering Project

Core Concept: The project aims to develop a portable nephelometer system that uses a smartphone camera to measure turbidity or suspended particle concentration in liquids through light scattering analysis. The system uses a low-cost optical setup, a sealed disposable micro-volume cartridge, and smartphone-based image processing to create an accessible and compact alternative to traditional laboratory nephelometers.

Traditional nephelometers used in laboratories are bulky, expensive, and require specialized instrumentation. This project addresses those limitations by designing a compact, smartphone-integrated nephelometer that uses controlled optical illumination and digital image processing to quantify scattered light intensity.

⚙️ System Architecture

  • Optical Illumination System: Monochromatic LED light source illuminates the liquid sample. Key design considerations include stable light intensity, fixed illumination geometry, minimal stray light, and consistent optical path.
  • Micro-Volume Sealed Sample Cartridge: Custom disposable sealed cartridge to prevent contamination, requiring small volume, with transparent optical windows and standardized geometry.
  • Opto-Mechanical Housing: 3D-printed enclosure to align the LED, sample, and smartphone camera (at a 90° scattering angle) while blocking ambient light and providing mechanical stability.
  • Smartphone Image Processing & Software: A custom pipeline to capture images, identify region of interest, extract pixel intensity, apply background correction, and quantify scattering intensity into turbidity metrics.

🎯 Key Project Goals / Design Objectives

  1. Optical Illumination Stability: Develop a consistent LED illumination system.
  2. Controlled Scattering Geometry: Maintain fixed optical angles (90°).
  3. Sealed Micro-Volume Sample Cartridge: Design a disposable cartridge that minimizes contamination.
  4. Opto-Mechanical Alignment: Create a 3D-printed housing that precisely aligns optical components.
  5. Ambient Light Isolation: Prevent external light interference through enclosure design.
  6. Smartphone-Based Detection: Utilize the smartphone camera as the primary optical detector.
  7. Image Processing Pipeline: Develop algorithms to quantify scattered light intensity.
  8. Calibration and Quantification: Establish a calibration curve linking measured intensity to turbidity.
  9. Low-Cost and Portable: Ensure the system is affordable, compact, and easy to deploy outside lab environments.
  10. Experimental Validation: Test the device using samples with known turbidity levels to validate measurement accuracy.

📊 Expected Outcome

The final system will demonstrate that a smartphone camera combined with controlled optical illumination and digital image processing can function as a practical nephelometric measurement device. The project aims to produce:

  • A functional prototype device
  • A sealed disposable micro-cartridge design
  • A smartphone-based measurement and analysis workflow
  • Experimental validation of turbidity measurement capability

This work contributes toward low-cost optical sensing and portable diagnostic instrumentation.
